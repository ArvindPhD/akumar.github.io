About

Advancing the frontiers of computational biology through innovative AI methodologies

I am a Principal Investigator and Associate Professor at Stanford University's Department of Biomedical Data Science, where I lead a research group focused on developing cutting-edge machine learning algorithms for understanding complex biological systems. My work bridges computational innovation with biological discovery, aiming to accelerate precision medicine and therapeutic development.

With over a decade of experience in computational biology, I have pioneered novel approaches in deep learning for genomic analysis, predictive modeling of disease progression, and multi-omics data integration. My research has been recognized with several prestigious awards and has been published in leading journals including Nature, Science, and Cell.

I am passionate about mentoring the next generation of computational biologists and am committed to open science practices. I serve on editorial boards of major journals and regularly review for top-tier conferences in both machine learning and computational biology.

Research Areas

Interdisciplinary approaches at the intersection of AI, biology, and medicine

Deep Learning for Genomics

Developing interpretable neural network architectures for predicting gene regulatory mechanisms, variant effect prediction, and discovering novel genomic patterns underlying disease.

Multi-Omics Integration

Creating computational frameworks that integrate genomic, transcriptomic, proteomic, and metabolomic data to provide comprehensive views of biological systems and disease states.

Precision Medicine

Building predictive models for patient stratification, treatment response prediction, and drug repurposing using large-scale clinical and molecular datasets.

Network Biology

Analyzing biological networks to understand cellular pathways, protein interactions, and disease mechanisms through graph neural networks and causal inference methods.

Single-Cell Analysis

Advancing computational methods for single-cell sequencing data analysis, cell type identification, and trajectory inference to understand cellular heterogeneity.

Biomarker Discovery

Employing machine learning for identifying novel biomarkers for early disease detection, prognosis, and therapeutic monitoring across various disease contexts.
